Revolutionizing Modern Medicine Through Computer Science

Photo by TVN24

By Carl Joshua Manese · July 22, 2025

Computer Science has become an essential part of many businesses and industries, but it’s especially significant to the progression of how we tackle healthcare and modern medicines. As of now, most computers have been used to help with understanding illnesses and healthcare solutions with the aid of digital models and electronic devices. However, with the rise of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technology, other applications have begun to rise and change the healthcare world forever.

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that deals with programming an algorithm that can learn information based on the amount of data that the engineers would feed them. AI is commonly used for recognizing speech patterns, making decisions based on the best outcomes, and as a translator for non-native languages. The ability of an AI to take in multiple data and come up with a prediction based on the symptoms makes it a great tool for healthcare applications.

Artificial Intelligence and Diagnosis

AI can be thought to learn and predict outcomes based on the common symptoms a patient may have. Unlike humans, AI is not burdened by biased opinions, prejudice, and laziness. Companies like AiCure have used AI to better understand the connection between a patient and their illnesses, while other companies like Insitro have used advanced machine learning to reduce the amount of time and money spent for diagnosing the required drug for each patient.

Computer Techniques and Healthcare

Keeping medicinal information secure and stored for a long time is important when it comes to keeping patients informed, and recording their previous illnesses for future diagnoses. A computerized information system for the hospital is a valuable tool for establishing easy access to the hospital’s data storage, transactions, and valuable patient information. Computers can also assist in making decisions to support doctors with the machine’s ability to have a vast memory and processing power. Computers may also assist in taking care of critically ill patients by keeping a close eye on them, where most humans would have been burdened by the loss of focus and being crowded with other tasks.

Ethical Concerns: Humans or Objects?

A critical issue that engineers must avoid is the ethical concerns that an AI may cross when performing a task. One of the ethical guidelines of the (High-Level Expert Group) HLEG for artificial intelligence is that AI has to respect the worth of a human’s life. It must never regard humans as objects it needs to fix, but as a human with emotions and beliefs. Engineers must make sure that their machine should learn to avoid hurting their patient’s emotions unless necessary. However, no amount of coding and technical workings would let the system understand and learn emotional intelligence, and so it remains important to have medical decisions made with AI be required to have a human assessment before any action is made.

To Conclude,

Advanced computers and Artificial Intelligence can be used as a valuable tool for healthcare technologies, as they can handle redundant tasks to free the staff to deal with more complicated tasks. Computers are also incapable of human burdens by having a consistently strong memory, unbiased nature, and longevity. However, we as humans must still remember the value of our humanity and remind ourselves that AI may never replace our sense of emotional values and ethical concerns. Overall, the future of healthcare will benefit with the addition of computer science and AI, provided it is supervised by the humans that made it.
